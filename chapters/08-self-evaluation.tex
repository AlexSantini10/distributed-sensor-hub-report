\section{Self-evaluation}\label{self-evaluation}

This chapter must be completed individually by each group member before final submission. To maintain a tone consistent with the rest of the report while clearly distinguishing personal responsibility for the content, a common structure is provided and should be replicated once for each student.

The objective of this section is not only to describe operational contribution, but also to provide a critical assessment of the work performed, honestly highlighting strengths, limitations that emerged during development, and the role effectively covered in the project.

\subsection{[First Name Last Name]}

\textbf{Role in the project.}
Describe concisely and objectively your predominant role within the group. Appropriate formulations may include protocol design, runtime implementation, development of automated tests, containerization management, web API development, implementation of the observability dashboard, coordination of final integration, or report writing.

\textbf{Main contributions.}
Report activities that were actually carried out, avoiding generic wording. It is preferable to describe concrete responsibilities, for example:
\begin{itemize}
  \item modules directly designed or implemented;
  \item automated tests developed or maintained;
  \item Docker scenarios or supporting scripts created;
  \item debugging, integration, and refinement activities;
  \item contributions to technical documentation and the final report.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Product strengths.}
Indicate aspects that, from your perspective, represent the best outcomes of the project. In this type of document, it is appropriate to focus on verifiable technical elements, such as architecture modularity, experiment reproducibility, observability clarity, solid test coverage, or correctness of convergence behavior.

\textbf{Weaknesses and limitations.}
This part should explicitly state real limitations of the system or development process. Relevant considerations include, for example, the absence of authentication, lack of durable persistence, dependence on simplified LWW policies, incomplete coverage of some extreme scenarios, or the need for manual tuning in denser topologies.

\textbf{Personal evaluation of contribution.}
Conclude with a brief critical reflection on your contribution: which decisions were most successful, which activities required more iterations, and what could have been improved in terms of design, testing, or coordination.

\textit{Note.}
The subsection above should be duplicated for each group member, replacing the placeholder \texttt{[First Name Last Name]} with actual names. Each student is exclusively responsible for their own part.
